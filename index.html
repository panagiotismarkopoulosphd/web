Expertise

Domains of Technical Expertise

  • Machine Learning, Deep Learning, and Artificial Neural Networks: supervised and unsupervised learning, anomaly detection, deep neural networks, representation learning, and model validation, performance evaluation, bias assessment, and algorithm audit.

  • Trustworthy/Robust AI/ML: model reliability under data corruption and adversarial contamination; outlier-resilient learning and tensor methods; stability under concept drift; analysis of AI system failures and data integrity issues.

  • Federated/Decentralized ML: on-device and distributed training under privacy constraints; non-i.i.d. and heterogeneous data; robustness in healthcare and sensing applications.

  • Computer Vision and Remote Sensing: object detection and classification in multimodal imagery; hyperspectral imaging; multimodal fusion and feature extraction.

  • Signal Processing and Communications: radar and remote sensing, wireless communication systems, sensor arrays, and time-varying data analysis.

  • Quantum-baesd ML: quantum annealing and hybrid quantum-classical algorithms for robust principal component and tensor analysis.

  • Security and surveillance: radar-based human activity recognition, target detection and tracking in multimodal remote sensing imagery, and information fusion from multiple modalities.

  • Healthcare and behavioral health: AI/ML models for healthcare decision support, behavioral health analytics, and privacy-preserving federated learning with clinical and patient data in privacy-sensitive, high-stakes healthcare contexts.

  • Wireless communication and sensing systems: signal processing, physical-layer design, antenna arrays, multiple channel access and interference avoidance/mitigation, secure and covert communication, time-varying and multimodal signals.

Professional/Academic Profile

Dr. Panagiotis (Panos) Markopoulos is an Associate Professor of Computer Engineering and Computer Science at The University of Texas at San Antonio and Cloud Technology Endowed Fellow. He is the founding director of the Machine Learning Optimization and Systems (MILOS) Laboratory and serves as Thrust Lead for Trustworthy AI in UT San Antonio’s MATRIX AI Consortium. His work spans machine learning and artificial intelligence (including supervised and unsupervised learning, anomaly detection, deep neural networks, and representation learning), trustworthy and robust AI, federated and decentralized learning, computer vision and remote sensing, signal processing and communications, and quantum and quantum-inspired optimization methods for AI systems. He has authored over 90 peer-reviewed publications and has served as principal investigator or co-principal investigator on federally funded research projects with sponsors including the National Science Foundation (NSF), the National Institutes of Health (NIH), the Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL), and the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency (NGA).

Overview and Metrics

Dr. Markopoulos is Founding Director of the Machine Learning Optimization and Systems (MILOS) Laboratory, leading UT San Antonio research in robust and efficient machine learning from challenging datasets, primarily in critical applications such as defense and healthcare. He serves as Thrust Lead for Trustworthy AI in the UT San Antonio AI Consortium (MATRIX) and as AI Systems Research Thrust Lead in the Department of Computer Engineering (2025–present). Granting Activities

Overview

Dr. Markopoulos’s research program has been supported by competitive funding from federal agencies, including the National Science Foundation, the U.S. Air Force Office of Scientific Research through its Young Investigator Program, the National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency, and the National Institutes of Health. He has served as sole Principal Investigator on multiple awards and as Lead Principal Investigator on several currently pending federal proposals in trustworthy federated learning, spectral optimization, and quantum tensor methods. In addition, he serves as Co-PI on large-scale efforts such as the NSF ExpandAI project NAIAD and on health-focused AI initiatives in the MATRIX consortium. As a MATRIX Thrust Lead, he drives interdisciplinary collaborative funding efforts across the main and medical campuses and helps shape institutional funding strategy through co-authorship of UT San Antonio’s responses to major federal requests for information and leadership in university cluster-hire efforts. His program has attracted approximately $5.8 million in total external project funding as PI/Co-PI across his career, with personal effort/share exceeding $1.68 million; more than 70% of this funding (over $4.1 million in new project totals, with personal effort/share exceeding $1.0 million) has been secured since his tenure in August 2021, including Co-PI leadership on the $2.8M NSF ExpandAI NAIAD project (15% effort, one of the largest AI grants at UT San Antonio).
